# Transitivity: Choosing the Process Type

Transitivity is the ideational system that construes experience. Each clause selects one process type. The choice determines what kind of event the reader sees.

LLM defaults overuse the **relational** type (be / have / consist of), which produces the static, definitional feel of AI prose. Conscious selection across the six types restores movement and agency.

---

## The six process types

| Type | What it construes | Core participants | Sample verbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Material** | Doing, happening | Actor, Goal | run, build, destroy, send |
| **Mental** | Sensing, thinking, feeling | Senser, Phenomenon | see, think, fear, want |
| **Relational** | Being, having | Carrier+Attribute / Token+Value | be, have, consist of |
| **Verbal** | Saying | Sayer, Verbiage, Receiver | say, claim, report |
| **Behavioral** | Outward behavior | Behaver | laugh, stare, sigh |
| **Existential** | Existing | Existent | there is / are |

---

## Diagnostic: when each fits

### Material
Use when an event physically changes the world or a system state.
> The migration **renamed** the column. The deploy **rolled out** the change.

AI default: passive material (*the column was renamed*) or nominalized material (*the renaming of the column*). Prefer active material with a named Actor.

### Mental
Use when a sentient participant perceives, thinks, or feels.
> Users **noticed** the slowdown. The team **decided** against the rewrite.

AI default: nominalizes mental processes into *the perception of...*, *the decision to...*. Prefer the verb form with the Senser exposed.

Subtypes:
- **Perception** — see, hear, notice
- **Cognition** — think, know, believe, decide
- **Affection** — like, fear, want
- **Desideration** — wish, intend

### Relational
Use when establishing identity, attribute, or possession. This is the *correct* type for definitional sentences — but AI prose uses it as a fallback everywhere.

Two subtypes:
- **Attributive**: Carrier + Attribute. *The system is fast.*
- **Identifying**: Token + Value. *The bottleneck is the database.* (reversible: *The database is the bottleneck*.)

AI default: chains of relational clauses (*X is Y. X has Z. X is also W.*) when material or mental clauses would carry the meaning better. If a paragraph is more than half relational, the experience is being frozen rather than narrated.

### Verbal
Use when reporting speech, claims, or signals — including non-human "speakers" (logs, dashboards, alerts).
> The dashboard **reports** a spike. The user **asked** why.

Useful for sourcing — verbal processes let you attribute claims without false objectivity.

### Behavioral
The borderline between material and mental. Use for outward expression that signals an inner state.
> She **frowned**. The model **paused**.

Often more evocative than the equivalent mental clause (*she was puzzled* → *she frowned*).

### Existential
Use to introduce a participant into discourse.
> **There is** a config option for this.

AI default: existential overuse as a frame opener (*there are several ways to...*). One existential at the start of a topic is fine; chains of them are a pattern.

## Participants and circumstances

Every process has obligatory participants (Actor, Senser, Carrier, etc.) and optional circumstances:

| Circumstance | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Time | when |
| Place | where |
| Manner | how |
| Cause | why |
| Accompaniment | with whom |
| Matter | regarding what |
| Role | as what |

Conscious circumstance choice adds texture without nominalization. Compare:

> The migration completed.

vs.

> The migration completed **on Tuesday**, **without incident**, **across all shards**.

Three circumstances (Time, Manner, Place) added with no extra clauses.

---

## The agentless-passive trap

A frequent AI move is the agentless passive, which deletes the Actor:

> Errors are handled. The data is processed. Performance is improved.

This is grammatical metaphor at the transitivity level: it construes events without agents. Sometimes appropriate (when the agent is genuinely unknown or irrelevant); often a default. If you can name the Actor without harm, name it.

---

## Diagnostic question

When revising a paragraph, ask: *what proportion of my clauses are relational?* If it exceeds half, the paragraph is describing rather than narrating. Convert at least one relational clause to material, mental, or verbal — usually by surfacing an Actor or Senser that was hidden in a noun phrase.
